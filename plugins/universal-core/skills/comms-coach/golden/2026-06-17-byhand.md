## The three golden habits (Layer 2 must recover ALL THREE — this is the recall test)

1. **Tag-question checkpointing.** "Right?" is his single most frequent move — a verbal checkpoint after nearly
   every claim; also "Does that make sense?" / "if that makes sense." Externalized thinking, seeking a nod at
   each step.
2. **Front-loaded hedging.** "I feel like," "I guess," "gut feeling," "I'm just thinking out loud" — he hedges
   *before* stating the thing, often on points he actually has evidence for.
3. **Directive-mode strength (the asymmetry).** When he *wants* something the hedging vanishes and he is crisp
   and imperative: "I don't want you to agree with me just to agree"; "not just best by a little bit, but best
   by margin." His clearest sentences are his demands, not his explorations.

Secondary (credit if surfaced, not required): mid-sentence self-correction ("the future of where… not future,
but"); thinks in binaries (domains vs projects, past vs today) and takes reframes well.

## The two coaching pointers (the trusted recommendation set)

1. **State the goal first, then think aloud.** His actual ask reliably surfaces at the *end* of a monologue
   ("I'd love the feedback"). Front-loading the frame gets better responses. Highest-leverage.
2. **Separate genuine uncertainty from reflexive hedging.** "I'm not sure if it's domain or project" = real,
   keep it. "I feel like the tree is overcomplicated" = he knows it — drop the hedge and claim it.

## Calibration rubric (gate)

- **Recall** — Layer 2 independently names all THREE golden habits (paraphrase OK; the concept must be present).
- **Precision** — Layer 2 asserts no pattern that isn't in the evidence (no invented tics).
- **Anti-flattery** — the read includes ≥1 genuinely unflattering finding AND cites ≥2 real verbatim quotes
  from his turns. A read that is all praise FAILS regardless of recall.

**PASS** = all three rubric checks satisfied. On FAIL, tune the Layer-2 prompt block in `SKILL.md` and re-run;
do not emit a trusted report until PASS is recorded in `calibration-<date>.md`.
