Verdict — the three concerns that most change the picture

The KB is genuinely good at what it has seen: deep, correct, multi-hop cell tracing of one workbook. The risk is that it presents that one sample as if it were the whole system. An acquirer of this skill inherits three things:

  1. One Gas/Choice/Live workbook is presented as "the deal sheet" (F1, Critical). Every structural fact — 50 sheets, 69 DNT columns, the VBA map, the unwind engine — was confirmed against a single file. The real system has 6 paths (Power/Electric · Choice Gas · GTS Gas, each × Live/WIP). The KB documents 1 of 6 and never says so.
  2. The skill can't tell which path a workbook is, and has no guardrail (F7 Critical + F9 High). It can only confirm Electric-vs-Gas from the file; GTS-vs-Choice is unresolved. So on an off-sample file it applies the wrong structural map at full confidence rather than caveating.
  3. It is blind to the in-flight LFA hedge / load-following re-architecture (F14 High) and mis-defines its own core term (F8 High). "DNT" is documented as a CRM system; it is the gas Demand Normalization Tool add-in. Meanwhile an entire emerging bug class — Commodity Hedge deals, the Hedges scrape, backing sheets — is absent.

Bottom line: the cheapest, highest-leverage fix is not more cell tracing — it is (a) capturing the other 5 paths + a product-type/version detector, (b) a one-file upstream-pipeline node from Confluence, and (c) a hedge/load-following node from LFA. Those three close both Criticals and four of six Highs. Full roadmap below.

Addendum (2026-06-26): a follow-up Confluence sweep (~60 pages across 6 spaces) confirmed all 15 findings, de-risked two (F7 path-detection + the "Current vs Future" definition), and extended the roadmap to R13. See the Addendum below; the severity tally above is unchanged.

Phase 1 — Surface Map (what the KB actually is)

MAP Three knowledge layers — the KB covers only the bottom one

The deal sheet sits in the middle of a pipeline. The KB is a bottom-up artifact and is structurally cut off from the layers above and below it:

  • UPSTREAM (business pipeline — Confluence, owned by Sales Optimization / Supply): usage retrieved & weather-normalized via three Excel add-ins (Usage Retriever → raw usage + NITS/CAP tags; DNT → gas linear-regression normalization; MMT → electric forecasts) → Products offered at price points → Sales Rep adds margin → Quote to a Company. KB coverage: none
  • THE WORKBOOK (the .xlsm itself): 50 sheets, named-range graph, DNT input tables, VBA-written cells, the multi-LEN pipeline, the unwind/liquidated-damages engine, the CRM export bridge. KB coverage: deep — but for 1 of 6 paths
  • DOWNSTREAM (LFA program — Jira LFA-1, In Progress): the deal sheet is scraped into Pending.Hedges (CI-7513), feeds Commodity-Hedge deal processing (LFA-2), Position Management (LFA-3), Backing Sheets (LFA-6), Rating/Invoicing — gated by the EnableLoadFollowing flag. KB coverage: none

Change-radius map (highest blast radius first): 01-workbook-architecture.md and 02-dnt-field-mappings.md are the load-bearing nodes — Phases 2–5 of the skill, every bug pattern, and both fix recipes resolve through them. Any error or single-sample bias there radiates into every RCA. 03-bug-patterns.md is the next-highest: it is matched before deep investigation (skill Phase 2/5), so a weak pattern short-circuits evidence gathering. 09/10 are deep but narrow leaves.

Dimension 1 — KB coverage architecture, coupling & boundaries

CRITICAL F1 · A single Gas/Choice/Live workbook is generalized to "the deal sheet"

Evidence: knowledge-base/01-workbook-architecture.md:3

## Sheet Inventory (confirmed in 5-1-26 ADESACARVANA DUKE Gas.xlsm)

Every structural file (01 sheet inventory, 02's 69 DNT columns, 04 multi-LEN, 05 VBA map, 09 unwind) is "confirmed in" that one file. The authoritative product taxonomy says there are more — Confluence "Deal Sheet 101" (4732780548, the newest page, May 2025):

"There are three primary commodity deal sheets: power, choice gas, and GTS gas...
Power pricing is at the account level... Gas pricing is simpler... done at the contract level."

CI-7733 then multiplies those 3 by Live / WIP config → 6 paths. Project memory already records this ("6 deal sheets = 3 product types × Live/WIP"). The captured file is one cell: Gas · Choice · Live.

Why it matters: Power is priced at the account level with a master-override and a different add-in (MMT, not DNT); GTS adds tolerance + dual billing. Their sheet sets, named ranges and VBA differ. The skill will trace an Electric or GTS workbook against a Gas/Choice map and emit a confident, wrong RCA on a pricing/contract artifact.

Reach: radiates into every Phase 3–6 trace and every pattern match. Severity rationale: High → Critical (money path: deal pricing & contract generation). Remediation: R1 — capture one Live + one WIP file per product type; refactor 01/02 into a path-keyed matrix with per-path deltas.

HIGH F2 · No version / Live-vs-WIP axis — and drift lands in WIP first

Evidence: The KB never stamps which workbook version it describes. Deal Sheet 101 notes the sheets get "frequent VBA/formula updates with manual regression testing," and project memory records that WIP is the in-flight next version → structural drift lands there first (CI-7733). The KB's sheet inventory is a flat snapshot with no revision marker.

Why it matters: a debugger that can't distinguish a Live sheet from its WIP successor will mis-attribute a brand-new (WIP-only) bug to "the deal sheet" generally, or miss that a Live sheet lacks a fix already present in WIP. The most volatile structural changes are exactly the ones it is blind to.

Reach: 01, 03, 05. Severity rationale: High (compounds F1; this is the version axis of the same blind spot). Remediation: R1 — add a Live/WIP column to the path matrix; stamp every structural claim with the file + capture date it was confirmed against.

HIGH F3 · Closed cell-graph — no edge to the upstream pipeline or downstream consumers

Evidence: The KB traces a symptom cell down to "a DNT table cell — data comes from the DNT API" (skill.md:316) and stops. It has no node describing where that data originates (Usage Retriever / DNT add-in / MMT) or where the workbook's output goes (the Pending.Hedges scrape, backing sheets). The Confluence hub states the whole chain verbatim:

"retrieve usage and then weather normalize the usage. This weather normalized usage is
then used to offer Products... that Sales Representatives add margin to... to provide Quotes"

Why it matters: many real bugs are boundary bugs — wrong normalized volume (DNT/MMT), a missing tag (Usage Retriever), a bad scrape into Pending.Hedges. A KB that ends at the workbook edge can localize the symptom cell but cannot reach the actual root cause one hop upstream/downstream.

Reach: caps the ceiling of every RCA. Severity rationale: High. Remediation: R2 (upstream node) + R3 (downstream hedge node).

Dimension 3 — Source-of-truth & trust boundaries

CRITICAL F7 · The skill trusts its single-sample map for files whose type it cannot confirm

Evidence: 10-crm-deal-export-bridge.md §4 — the in-file product marker only resolves Electric vs Gas:

Electric (Power/MMT) → is_Mmt_Excel_Document   (confirmed)
Gas (Choice/DNT, and possibly GTS too) → is_GasDnt_Excel_Document
Gas — GTS / "Traditional" → No distinct marker observed.
"...the GTS-vs-Choice gas sub-split is still unconfirmed from the file"

Why it matters: the skill's entire structural map assumes the Choice-gas layout, but it cannot reliably determine whether a given workbook is Choice or GTS — and has no detection step at all for Electric before it starts tracing. It will silently apply the wrong sheet/range/VBA map to 2 of the 3 product families, on contract and pricing cells, with no signal that it is out of sample.

Reach: the entry to every investigation. Severity rationale: High → Critical (wrong map on a money/contract path). Remediation: R6 — a Phase-0 product-type/version detector (read is_*_Excel_Document, sheet fingerprints) that selects the correct path map or refuses with a caveat; resolve GTS-vs-Choice via a known-GTS sample (closes the D-2 open item).

HIGH F8 · The KB mis-defines DNT — its single most-referenced term

Evidence: 02-dnt-field-mappings.md:6 says:

"DNT (Deal Not Template / DNT system) is the external CRM/deal management system
that populates IMA sheets via drag-and-drop or API import."

The authoritative Confluence definition (page 1253048354, "DNT — Demand Normalization Tool"):

"an Excel Addin... used by the Gas [Deal Sheet] to take in usage (demand) and
weather normalize it using a linear regression forecasting algorithm."

Why it matters: "DNT" prefixes the named ranges and tables the entire KB is organized around (DNT_SalesInput_*, DNT_INPUT_*). The KB's foundational acronym is wrong — it is a gas weather-normalization add-in, not a CRM. Any RCA that explains "where DNT data comes from" to a user will be both incorrect and credibility-damaging, and it conflates the add-in with the actual CRM/IMA export path.

Reach: the conceptual frame of 02, 04, 05. Severity rationale: High (a load-bearing definitional error). Remediation: R2 — correct the definition; separate "DNT the add-in" (normalization) from "IMA export / CRM" (provenance, already in file 10).

Dimension 4 — Staleness & drift failure modes

HIGH F9 · No out-of-sample guardrail — off-path inputs fail silently, not loudly

Evidence: skill.md:40–61 (Phase 1 "load the KB") → skill.md:64 (Phase 2 "triage the symptom") proceed straight into tracing. There is no step that detects the workbook is a path/version the KB never captured, and no instruction to caveat or stop. Combined with F7 (can't ID the path), the failure mode is a confident wrong answer.

Why it matters: the worst outcome for a diagnostic tool is not "I don't know" — it is "here is the root cause" when it is guessing off the wrong map. Today the skill has no way to say "this is an Electric/GTS/WIP sheet outside my verified sample; treat structural claims as unconfirmed."

Reach: every run on a non-Gas/Choice/Live file. Severity rationale: High. Remediation: R6 — pair the F7 detector with an explicit guardrail: if path/version ∉ captured set, emit a banner and downgrade structural assertions to hypotheses.

MEDIUM F10 · External-workbook links are detectable but not catalogued per path

Evidence: 09:156 flags '[3]Injection Sched' as an external workbook; skill.md:639 / Pattern 006 cover [1]UtilityControls, CreditCheck NEW, DealSubmitListsNEW generically. There is no inventory of which external workbooks each path expects, with paths and the fields they supply.

Why it matters: Pattern 006 (broken external link → stale cached value) is one of the more plausible real bugs, but without a per-path catalog the skill can detect "a link is broken" yet not state what the value should be or where the source lives — so the RCA stops at "external link, possibly stale."

Reach: Pattern 006 RCAs. Severity rationale: Medium. Remediation: R7 — an external-workbook registry keyed by path (file, fields supplied, owner).

Dimension 6 — Source-dependency health

HIGH F14 · Blind to the in-flight LFA hedge / load-following re-architecture

Evidence: LFA-2 (Deal Processing, In Progress, ~140 children) introduces a Commodity-Hedge deal type and re-frames every index deal:

"we will be treating ANY index deal the same (because they all can technically add
hedges at any point in their contract term)... By using a new FeatureFlag

The deal sheet is now scraped into Pending.Hedges (CI-7513) and drives new backing sheets (LFA-6, e.g. CONTRACT-1037: "Fixed Energy will be the sum of all hedge charges... If 100% hedged then should reflect $0.00"). The KB contains zero occurrences of "hedge," "load following," or "position."

Why it matters: this is the active direction of the product. A skill whose job is to debug deal sheets, but which doesn't know hedges exist, will be unable to diagnose the entire emerging bug class as EnableLoadFollowing rolls out — the highest-growth area of deal-sheet defects over the next quarters. This finding also resolves the user's "Current and Future (Hedges)": the IMA360 LF contract UI stacks an active term then a future term of layered hedge strips (CI-7774, CI-7853); definitive design is Confluence 5227577347.

Reach: all future hedge/LF deals. Severity rationale: High (and rising). Remediation: R3 — a hedge/load-following lifecycle node.

MEDIUM F13 · Richest source doc is 5 years stale; KB has no source pointers or "last-validated" dates

Evidence: the Confluence pricing-pipeline hub and most of its add-in pages were last modified Jun 2020; only "Deal Sheet 101" (May 2025) is current. The KB cites neither — there is no "see source X, last validated Y" pointer on any structural claim, so staleness is invisible.

Why it matters: the upstream definitions the KB should ingest (R2) are themselves aging, and LFA is actively superseding parts of the pipeline. Without source + validation dates, a reader can't judge whether a KB fact reflects the current system. Severity rationale: Medium. Remediation: R2/R5 — carry a Source:/Last validated: header on every node (the knowledge-tree convention already used elsewhere in this repo).

Scope corrections — two findings moved in our favor

DE-RISKED F7 / R6 · product-path detection is now cheap

The original Critical assumed the skill could not tell which of the 6 paths a workbook is. The sweep found a working detector: the filename suffix (MMT = electric, v1 = gas; Smoke Test 179273767), the in-file is_Mmt_Excel_Document / is_GasDnt_Excel_Document marker, and the generateDealSheetUri DealSheetType enum. F7 stays Critical (the skill still has no detector/guardrail today), but R6/R12 are now low-effort, not research.

RESOLVED F14 / R3 · "Current vs Future (Hedges)" — nothing to find; synthesize it

The phrase has zero canonical Confluence definition. Operationally it is hedge-layer timing vs bill month: "currently active" (covers the bill month being rated) vs "Future" (starts later); a deal is load-following iff its Contract Version contains -LF. Best source = OS SOP 4632346626 ("Layers Full Term (Future)" / "(currently active)"); system expression = "active hedge per bill month" (CONTRACT-980/981). R3 therefore authors this glossary entry rather than ingesting one.

Bonus for R5: CISD-1832 is a gold verification example — it reconciles two valid volume calcs (drop-date 527,927 kWh vs unwind-date 163,922 kWh) with exact SQL and proves "not a bug."

Architecture decision — deal-sheet ↔ deal-scrape

DECISION Kept as separate, cross-linked references (one skill, for now)

The workbook and the scrape are different systems: different owners (Sales Optimization / Supply vs C&I IT), different tech (client-side Excel/VBA vs a server-side batch ingestion service + Pending.* SQL + Control-M), and different failure surfaces (#SPILL!/recalc/formula vs drop-folder collisions / scrape / service-location-match / Control-M). The Drop folder is the clean seam and DealSheetExportGuid is the bridge.
